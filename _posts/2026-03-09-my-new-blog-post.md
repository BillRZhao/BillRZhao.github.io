## The Reasons Why I Like Econometrics

The reason why I like econometrics is that the way this subject thinks about the world highly
aligns with my own. We view this world as a data generating process (DGP), which is usually a
black box. We assume some distributions on the observed and unobserved random variables
based on the theories that we know. Then, based on those distributions and the observations that
we can get, we analyze what parameters are identifiable, and we develop methods to estimate
them and do inference on our estimates to see whether they are credible. By doing this, our
theories are tested.

The reason why econometric research is interesting is that each step above generates a lot of
topics for us to research. When we model the DGP using distributions, we face the problem of
specification, that is, whether the model is correctly specified and how to test this. When we
have correctly specified models, we face the problem of identification, that is, whether our
parameter of interest can be recovered from the distributions of random variables that we can
observe. At the estimation step, we need to develop estimators with desirable properties such as
consistency and efficiency. We also need to examine their credibility using statistical inference
methods.

A criterion for a subject to be called “science” is whether its models and conclusions are
falsifiable by observations. Without econometrics, the subject of economics would only be a
groundless philosophical contemplation. With econometrics, it becomes a real science. This is
why I believe that econometric research is highly valuable.
Apart from all those objective values of econometrics, two personal reasons why I like it are the
following. First, I enjoy the feeling of purity, intensity, and eternal consistency when I
contemplate mathematical objects. Econometric theory provides me with a great ground to do
this within the broad category of economic science. Second, it seems that the skills that I can
learn from doing econometrics are highly transferrable into the skills preferred by the industry,
which means that, with the academic job market becoming increasingly competitive, I could find
myself a way out, a second path.

Thus is a personal testimony of my fondness for econometrics.
